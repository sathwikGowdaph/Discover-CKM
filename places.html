Popular Places

Baba Budangiri

Baba Budangiri peak is a sacred mountain in the Chikmagalur district of Karnataka, named after the revered Sufi saint Baba Budan. The range is known for its picturesque landscapes, trekking trails, and being the historical origin of coffee in India, introduced by Baba Budan in the 17th century. The site is also a pilgrimage destination for both Hindus and Muslims, with a shared shrine and cave complex called Datta Peetha.

Kudremukh

Kudremukh peak is the second-highest peak in Karnataka and the highest in the Chikmagalur district, located at an altitude of 6,207 feet (1,894 meters) within the Kudremukh National Park in the Western Ghats. The peak is named "Kudremukh" (horse's face) due to its distinctive horse-face-like shape, and it is a popular destination for trekking through its beautiful rolling hills, grasslands, and shola forests, offering rich biodiversity and the chance to spot wildlife like deer and peacocks.

Z Point

Z Point in Chikmagalur is a mesmerizing trekking destination that offers panoramic views of the Western Ghats. Located near Kemmangundi, this vantage point provides an ideal spot for adventure lovers and nature enthusiasts.
